Belly Button Biodiversity Dashboard

Select a Test Subject ID to explore belly button data!

I bet you would never guess that scientifically, your belly button is defined as a habitat! And yet, it is remains relatively unexplored… (Except of course, by Ms. Frizzle in season 1 episode 3 of The Magic School Bus, where she takes her class on a trip inside the human body.) However - the Belly Button Biodiversity Study is taking it a step further! (Sorry Ms. Frizzle… )

In 2011, the study was launched by The Public Science Lab at North Carolina State University to investigate the microbes inhabiting our navels. The study revealed that a small handful of microbial species (also called Operational Taxonomic Units, or OTUs) were present in more than 70% of the test subjects.

To view data on each of the subject’s results, I put together a few dynamic visualizations using Plotly’s JavaScript graphing library. Go ahead and choose a Test Subject ID to start exploring the data. It may not be as exciting as Ms. Frizzle’s magic school bus, but hey, data is data! And it must be explored none-the-less!
